Youthguard

Educational platform for young Australians about government finances. A website and app that tracks how tax dollars are spent and educates youth about financial decisions that directly affect them.

Addressing Youth Disenfranchisement

This brief required addressing a specific problem area of a large scale issue. The chosen issue is youth disenfranchisement with the Australian government, specifically focusing on government spending.

The response was a website and app that educates younger people about Australia's economy, government financial decisions that directly affect them, and tracks how their taxes are being spent.

By providing transparency and education, Youthguard aims to bridge the gap between young Australians and their government, fostering civic engagement through understanding.

Trustworthy Brand Identity

The first step was creating a brand identity that feels modern and trusting. The Youthguard brandmark uses a shield in blue, visually displaying the goal of providing protection to youth in terms of information about their tax dollars.

This shield is represented in blue as blue is a trusting color. The brandmark uses Indivisible by Eric Olsen, which is a variable sans serif font that conveys modernity and accessibility.
