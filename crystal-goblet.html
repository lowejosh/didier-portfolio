CRYSTAL GOBLET

Three pieces of literature were provided, with the task to make typographic decisions that reflect the message & tone of each literature piece. The deliverables consisted of forming all three pieces into a cohesive book/ booklet.

The illustations are tied in with the title of each pieces whilst simultaniously used to create a cohesive theme throughout the book. Resulting in a design that is both unique and appropriate for the project requirements.

ILLUSTRATIONS

Illustrations were not a requirement of the brief but it felt like a positive design choice. Adding uniqueness and character to the book layout by creating abstract designs that draw readers in, whilst working within constraints of letterforms and overarching shapes.

The designs seek to tie in each literature piece with a common thematic. The theme chosen for this project was snowy landscapes, often used to represent a sense of wonder, remoteness and freedom.

page illustrations

typography as discourse

Typography as discourse by Katherine McCoy & Davud Frej looks at the evolution of typography through the past century through the lens of graphic designers. Although once revolutionary, New Wave became mainstream, leading to new explorations in design focused on expression, content, and the interplay between text and imagery.

This is where the idea to interplay text and imagery in the layout came from. The flower was chosen as the overarching illustation because the literature looks at how typography grows and changes. The display typeface used is Barricada pro by Eli Castellanos due to its flowy feel and 70's asthetic, appropriate for a flower.

the science of typography

The science of typography looks at data from different studies about the most optimal typographic choice one can make to ensure readability. Consequently I followed the conclusions presented in the literature, using a san serif with a high x-height, 10pt font, and a maximum of 40 characters per line. This theme was carried throughout the body texts of all 3 literature pieces using Museo Sans by Jos Buivenga. The display font for this piece was Temerair Display by quentin Schmerber due to its characterful yet professional asthetic.

the crystal goblet

The Crystal Goblet by Beatrice Warde is about how good typography is invisible. Typefaces are expressive with large amounts of variation between families. Beatrice states that a mismatch between typeface and literature takes away value from both, it must be appropriate for the context. This is a lesson I carry throughout all my designs.

The display typeface is Lust Didone by Neil Summerour, it is a highly expressive and charasmatic typeface that reflects the literatures context well.
